The Arabian Sea

Why is it a great home for coral?!

Introduction:

Healthy Corals are the foundation of our oceans food chain. Healthy coral reefs contribute to fishing and tourism, providing millions of jobs and contributing to economies all over the world. Scientists develop important drugs from coral reef organisms as treatments for cancer and viruses. However, corals are threatened by pollution and climate change. Our human activity causes pollution levels to increase and climate change to occur quicker. It is gravely important to protect the coral reefs.

This shows that coral is in fact present in the Arabian Sea. I think that this is relevant to us all in a round-about way; the factors that determine the presence or absence of corals are controlled by human activity. If we learn more about these factors, maybe we can prevent the death of coral reefs in the future. The main goal of my project is to analyse the factors that can affect the distribution of corals in the Arabian Sea. Seeing as corals are present and thriving in this environment, the factors of the Arabian Sea can be copied and possibly implemented into other seas. This may be done by reducing pollution and slowing down the rate of climate change. Overall, my project may assist in indicating what humanity can do to save coral reefs ecosystem.
