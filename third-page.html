About the dataset:

My data represents the factors of the Arabian Sea, factors that are healthy for coral reef life. The salinity refers to the practical salinity units of the Arabian Sea water surface. In other words, it can also be defined as the salt concentration of the water. The temperature refers to the temperature of the Arabian Sea water surface in degrees Celsius. The corals present simply refer to whether or not corals are present in the sea. In this case, yes, corals are present in the Arabian Sea.

Both the temperature and the salinity are the variables as they were measured in the two months January and June. The temperature and salinity are both a float data type. The corals present are a constant figure throughout the dataset. They are the boolean data type.
